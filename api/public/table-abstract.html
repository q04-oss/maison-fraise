what it is

Table is a feature inside box fraise — a platform built around the idea that the most valuable thing a technology company can capture is the moment a person is physically present somewhere.

Table is where that happens. A small group of guests pays to sit with a member of your staff and learn something. The session is intimate, the format is fixed, and the booking is handled entirely by the platform. Your job is to show up and do the thing you already know how to do.

the format

Sessions are small by design. Two guests per staff member, four guests maximum.

how it works

A card on your counter has a QR code. A customer scans it, lands on your venue's page, and pays to hold a spot. No app required. No account needed at the point of booking.

Sessions are listed without a fixed date. The date is set once seats fill. If a guest can't make the confirmed date, they get a full refund. If more people want in than the session holds, they carry forward automatically into the next run — already confirmed, no re-booking required.

The cycle repeats as long as guests keep scanning. There is no end date.

revenue

Box fraise collects payment at checkout and distributes as follows:

your business 85%
box fraise 15%

Of your 85%, the structure this program is built around is 65% to the staff member who led the session and 20% retained by the business. How you compensate your staff is your decision — this is what we communicate as the intended split and what the platform is designed around.

what this means for your staff

The employee leading a session is not doing a favour for the business. They are the product. Table is built on the premise that the person with the skill deserves the majority of what that skill earns. The 65% expectation is not arbitrary — it is the position of the platform.

A group session at CA$150 per guest with 4 guests generates CA$600. CA$510 goes to your business, of which CA$390 is the staff member's share. CA$90 goes to box fraise.

the broader picture

Box fraise is a platform that treats physical presence as a credential. When a guest books through table, pays, and attends, their account on the platform reflects that. They were there. The platform knows it. That record is the beginning of an identity system that does not depend on usernames or passwords.

Being on table means your venue is part of that system. Guests who find you through a session may have first encountered the platform through a different venue entirely. As more businesses join, every listing benefits from the audience built by every other listing.
