table · a venue-hosted experience program

premise

Table is a small-group experience program that lives inside box fraise, a platform built around taste. It turns your venue into an intimate classroom — not by bringing in outside instructors, but by letting your own staff lead.

Your team already knows how to do things most people never will. Table creates a structured way to share that knowledge with guests who want to learn it, in small groups, in the space where the work actually happens.

format

Sessions are deliberately small. Intimacy is the product.

group

up to 4 guests
2 per staff member
max 2 staff
standard pricing

private

2 guests
1 staff member
exclusive session
premium pricing

The private format is designed for couples or pairs who want the full attention of one person. It commands a higher price and runs as a standalone booking — no shared session, no strangers at the counter.

how booking works

A card sits on your counter with a QR code. A customer scans it, lands on your venue's page inside the box fraise platform, and pays to reserve a spot. No app required. No account needed. Just a phone and a card.

Events are listed without a fixed date. The date is confirmed once seats fill. If a guest can't make the confirmed date, they get a full refund. If demand exceeds capacity, the overflow carries forward automatically into the next run — guests who paid are already confirmed, with no re-booking required.

The cycle is indefinite. As long as guests keep scanning the QR code, sessions keep running.

revenue

box fraise collects payment at checkout and distributes as follows:

your business 85%
box fraise 15%

Of your 85%, the intended structure is: 65% to the employee who led the session, 20% retained by the business as a venue fee. How you compensate your staff for their time is your decision — but that is the split this program is designed around, and what we communicate to participating businesses.

A group session priced at CA$150 with 4 guests generates CA$510 in gross revenue: CA$433 to your business (of which CA$390 is intended for the employee), CA$77 to fraise.

the broader context

box fraise is a platform for people who care about how things are made — food, objects, craft. Table is one feature inside that platform. It is not a marketplace, not an events listing, not a class aggregator. It is a specific program with a specific structure, built for venues that already have something worth sharing.

Edmonton has those venues. This is the mechanism.

As the platform grows, so does the audience. Every business listed on box fraise benefits from the traffic generated by every other business on it. A guest who books a session at your venue may have found you through a completely different listing — and vice versa. Being on table means being visible to everyone already on the platform, not just the customers who already know you.
